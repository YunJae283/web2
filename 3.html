Cactuses are xerophytes, which means they are adapted to the desert conditions. These plants vary in size and generally do not have any branches or leaves. Cactuses generally have spines that range from small hairlike projections to large spikes. Xerophytes, like cactuses, grow successfully indoors and outdoors in other climates when their needs are met.
